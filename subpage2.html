Siam Road Charcoal Char Koay Teow

Char Koay Teow, which translates to "stir-fried rice noodles," is a savory dish traditionally made with flat rice noodles (kway teow) stir-fried in a wok over high heat. It is a hawker favorite, known for its smoky aroma and bold flavors.

Opening hours: Tuesday to Saturday (12:00 to 18:00), Closed on Sundays & Mondays
Price: RM 1-20

Lao San Tong Bak Kut Teh

Bak Kut Teh is a popular and aromatic Chinese herbal soup, often considered a comforting dish that is perfect for cooler weather or as a nourishing meal. The name "Bak Kut Teh" translates to "meat bone tea" in Hokkien, referring to the tender meat and flavorful herbal broth that defines this iconic dish. Though originally from the southern regions of China, it has become an integral part of Malaysian and Singaporean cuisine, with Penang offering its own local variations.

Opening hours: 16:00 to 22:00 , Closed on Wednesdays
Price: RM 20-40

Open Location

Sin Nam Huat Chicken Rice

Chicken Rice is a popular and comforting dish in Malaysian and Singaporean cuisine, often regarded as one of the staples in both countries. With its simple yet flavorful ingredients, it’s a perfect balance of tender chicken, aromatic rice, and flavorful accompaniments. The dish is inspired by Chinese Hainanese cooking, but over time, it has evolved into a local favorite in Penang and throughout Southeast Asia.

Opening hours: 09:30 to 16:00 , Closed on Sundays
Price: RM 1-20

Hamid Satay

Satay in Penang is a popular and flavorful street food, enjoyed by locals and visitors alike. The dish, a legacy of Malaysia's rich cultural blend, is typically made with skewered meats, grilled over an open flame, and served with a signature peanut sauce. In Penang, you’ll find many unique twists on this classic dish, with various types of meat, side accompaniments, and preparation methods.

Opening hours: 17:30 to 23:00 , Closed on Sundays & Mondays
Price: RM 1-20
